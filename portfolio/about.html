Hi, I'm Choksi.

A Creative Technologist who goes by his last name.

Photo by Jordan Ashleigh
When a writer says "the interaction just feels… off," I add a 300ms silent audio file before the punchline. When a computer vision model confuses a T-Rex cartoon with a Stegosaurus, I figure out a new object labeling strategy.

I've been coding since 11, and never stopped. After graduating with a degree in Information Systems from UT Austin, I spent a decade in eLearning and web development. All the while, I spent nights performing improv and stand-up comedy. That led to 1,000+ live shows, writing under Mo Willems, and an Emmy nomination for Daniel Tiger's Neighborhood.

At Kibeam Learning, I put it all together. Kids point a wand at picture books, and the books talk back — powered by computer vision, custom audio hardware, and a lot of XML. I led implementation on 20+ titles and built tools that translated "Make it more magical" into code an engineer could ship by Tuesday.

"Would I want my daughter to use this?"

That's the barometer. Screenless products. Physical play.

If you're building for kids, I'd love to talk.
