Hi, I'm Choksi.

A Creative Technologist who goes by his last name.

Photo by Jordan Ashleigh
When a writer says "the interaction just feels… off," I add a 300ms silent audio file before the punchline. When a computer vision model confuses a T-Rex illustration with a Velociraptor's, I figure out a new object labeling strategy.

I've been coding since 11, and never stopped. Information Systems degree from UT Austin, then years building eLearning at Ford Motor and front-end systems for Chicago startups. All the while, I spent nights performing improv and stand-up comedy. That led to 1,000+ live shows, a gig hosting a nightly TV talk show, a fellowship with Sesame Workshop, writing under Mo Willems, and an Emmy nomination for Daniel Tiger's Neighborhood.

Then my daughter was born. Everywhere, I saw technology fighting for her attention. I decided to fight back.

Now I translate. "Make it more magical" becomes code an engineer can ship by Tuesday. At Kibeam Learning, I led implementation on 20+ interactive children's books using custom audio hardware, sensors, and computer vision.

"Would I want my daughter to use this?"

That's the barometer. Screenless products. Physical play. Tech that enables instead of extracts.

If you're building for kids, I'd love to talk.
